002 : Tourist spots

　　　France is one of the most popular countries in Europe for sightseeing tours because of its many tourist attractions. Not only does it have a large number of World Heritage sites, but it is also famous for its gastronomy, and the food is delicious. In this article, I introduce four spots that I have carefully selected.

Eiffel Tower

　　　Paris is the number one city for tourism in France, and its symbol, the Eiffel Tower, is a tourist attraction that tops the list of famous buildings in France. built in conjunction with the 1889 World Exposition, the Eiffel Tower celebrates its 130th anniversary in 2019. The Eiffel Tower can be seen from every corner of Paris, and is a sight to behold both day and night. 324 meters high, it has three observation decks, and the top floor offers a 360-degree panoramic view of Paris.

Mont Saint-Michel

　　　Mont Saint-Michel is located at the base of the Brittany Peninsula in northwestern France. In France, which has many World Heritage Sites, it is one of the most popular and highly rated. The monastery is so beautiful that pilgrims in the Middle Ages were said to have risked their lives to reach it.

Versailles

　　　The Palace of Versailles, the most luxurious palace in the world and a symbol of absolute monarchy in Europe, is one of the most popular tourist attractions you must visit during your trip to Paris. Built in 1682 by Louis XIV, also known as the Sun King, the palace was registered as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1979. In addition to the interior of the palace, famous for the Hall of Mirrors, there is also the Fountain Square with the Latona Fountain, Marie Antoinette's hamlet, and the Grand Trianon Palace.

The Arc de Triomphe

　　　The Arc de Triomphe is the starting point of the Champs-Élysées boulevard and is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Paris. Named the Arc de Triomphe, it was built to commemorate Napoleon Bonaparte's victory at the Battle of Austerlitz. Standing in the center of Charles de Gaulle Square, this gate is a symbol of the glory of France itself. One of the twelve streets leading from the gate is the famous Champs-Élysées. You can enjoy its greatness during the day, but it is worth seeing at night when it is beautifully lit up.
